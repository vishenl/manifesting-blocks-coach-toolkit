Client Language Cheat Sheet

Listen for these phrases — they signal which block is active.

1. Upper Limit Problem
  • "I don't have the education / skills / background for that."
  • "I'm not ready yet — I need more training first."
  • "What if I fail? What if it doesn't work out?"
2. Emotional Incongruence
  • "I don't think I deserve that."
  • "Good things don't happen to people like me."
  • "Every time something good happens, something bad follows."
  • "I feel guilty when I receive compliments / gifts / money."
3. Fear of Success
  • "What if I lose myself?"
  • "What if my friends leave me when I outgrow them?"
  • "If I get there, I'll have to deal with [taxes / employees / responsibility]."
  • "What if I have to hustle insanely hard to sustain it?"
4. Hustle Mode
  • "I have to work harder / grind more."
  • "If I stop pushing, everything will fall apart."
  • "I feel restless / anxious when I'm not doing something."
  • "I feel guilty when I rest."
5. Wrong Environment
  • "My home / office feels heavy / draining."
  • "The people around me don't support my dreams."
  • "I feel stuck but I don't know why."
  • "Everyone around me is negative / gossiping / fearful."
6. Bullshit Rules (Brules)
  • "Money is dangerous / dirty / the root of all evil."
  • "Rich people are bad / greedy."
  • "People like me don't get to have that."
  • "My parents always said…"
  • "It's not safe to be visible / wealthy / successful."
7. Visualization from Lack
  • "I visualize every day but nothing changes."
  • "I can see it but it feels so far away."
  • "Looking at my vision board makes me feel sad / frustrated."
  • "I want it so badly."
8. Time Lag Frustration
  • "I've been doing this for months and nothing is happening."
  • "I'm running out of time."
  • "Maybe this manifesting stuff doesn't work."
  • "I should have had this by now."
9. Surrender Block
  • "But when will it happen?"
  • "I need it to happen THIS way."
  • "Why is the universe ignoring me?"
  • "I've done everything right, where are my results?"
10. Quantum Leap Syndrome
  • "Everything is happening so fast, I can't keep up."
  • "I got what I wanted and now I'm panicking."
  • "This feels too good to be true."
  • "I'm scared I'll mess it up now that I have it."
  • "I feel like a fraud / imposter."
DETAILED GUIDE

In-depth coaching reference for each of the 10 manifesting blocks

Block 1
The Car · Inner Identity

The Upper Limit Problem

A glass ceiling on what you believe you're capable of. The goal feels unfamiliar, and you unconsciously hold yourself back from reaching it.
Core Belief "I don't have what it takes to get there."

How to Recognize It

  • Client uses language of lack: "I don't have the education / skills / background."
  • Nervousness and "what if" fears arise when thinking about a big goal.
  • Self-sabotage when approaching the ceiling — avoidance of opportunities.
  • Defaulting to "I'm not ready" or "I need more training first."
  • This block is domain-specific: confident in one area, blocked in another.

Common Origins

  • Childhood wounds and cultural rules about capability
  • Discouraging teachers, parents, or authority figures
  • A single defining comment that became embedded (e.g., being told "you suck at X" at a formative age)

Suggested Exercises

1. The Self-Check Question

Ask the client: "Do you truly, 100%, feel you have what it takes to achieve [goal]?" If there's any hesitation, the upper limit is present. Have them write down exactly what they feel they lack.

2. Expand Emotional Capacity for Joy

Have the client repeat daily: "I am safe to feel this good" and "I feel safe to feel this good." This gradually raises the internal thermostat for what they allow themselves to experience.

3. Evidence Journaling (3 minutes)

Each morning, find one piece of evidence from the past day or week that confirms they are already on the path. Accumulating evidence chips away at the glass ceiling over time.

Block 2
The Car · Inner Identity

Emotional Incongruence

A worthiness block — even when good things arrive, the nervous system pulls the client back to "safety." Unlike the upper limit (capability), this is about deserving.
Core Belief "I don't deserve this."

How to Recognize It

  • Self-sabotage at the moment of receiving — getting sick after a promotion, fights after moving in with a partner.
  • Physical symptoms emerging at moments of success.
  • Guilt around money or success; difficulty accepting compliments.
  • "Every time something good happens, something bad follows."
  • Pattern of giving away or undermining their own success.

Common Origins

  • Deep childhood wounds creating a core story of unworthiness
  • Guilt, stigma, or shame from past experiences
  • Early separation or abandonment defining relationship with receiving

Suggested Exercises

1. Inner Child Healing

Guide the client to visualize their inner child. Have them hold that child and say: "You are safe now." This begins rewiring the nervous system's safety threshold for receiving.

2. Daily Mirror Ritual

Each morning, look at one eye in the mirror and repeat: "You are worthy of this" or "I love you, I love you, I love you." (Kamal Ravikant technique.) Consistency matters more than intensity.

3. The Receiving Question

Ask: "If your goal showed up tomorrow, could you receive it with ease?" For money: What would it take to claim it without guilt? For love: What would you need to say or confess? Explore what stands between them and graceful receiving.

Block 3
The Car · Inner Identity

Fear of Success

The sneaky one — the client thinks they fear failure, but they actually fear what success demands: lifestyle changes, responsibility, exposure, outgrowing loved ones.
Core Belief "I can't sustain it once I get there." / "I'm afraid of who I'll have to become."

How to Recognize It

  • Getting close to a major goal and then pulling back or self-sabotaging.
  • Fear language about the consequences of success: "What if I lose my friends?" / "What if I have to work even harder?"
  • Destroying relationships once they become "real."
  • Avoiding commitments that would lead to the goal.

Common Origins

  • Childhood associations with success symbols (e.g., a big house meaning financial stress)
  • Fear of public scrutiny or responsibility
  • Watching parents or role models struggle under the weight of success

Suggested Exercises

1. Redefine Success as Freedom

Have the client rewrite their goal to include freedom rather than pressure. Example: Not "build a company" but "build a company with just five people, in the leanest way possible, working no more than two hours a day." Remove the fear by redesigning the destination.

2. Visualize Expansion with Joy

Guide a visualization where everything unfolds with "supreme ease" — people coming to them, doors opening, things moving with joy rather than burnout. Anchor success to soul mission, not ego.

3. The "Include Them" Reframe

If they fear outgrowing loved ones, brainstorm ways to bring those people along: internships, free tickets, invitations, shared experiences. Success doesn't have to mean leaving people behind.

Block 4
The Fuel · Energetic Alignment

Hustle Mode

Operating from lack — discipline replaces intuition, the client chases rather than attracts. The shadow side of the achiever archetype.
Core Belief "If I don't push, it won't come." / "I must make it happen."

How to Recognize It

  • Drained, restless, anxious when NOT doing something.
  • Guilt when resting; uses pressure rather than inspiration to motivate.
  • Nervous system constantly in "go mode."
  • Work feels like hard work rather than a natural next step.
  • Chasing synchronicities rather than receiving them.

Common Origins

  • Achiever archetype conditioning — culture of hustle
  • Core fear that stopping means losing everything
  • Identity wrapped up in productivity and doing

Suggested Exercises

1. The Alignment Mantras

Have the client post these where they'll see them daily:
"I no longer chase. I attract.
I no longer prove. I align.
I no longer hustle. I flow.
My energy is my strategy."

2. The Reframe Practice (3 minutes)

When in hustle mode, think of the most stressful item on the calendar. Ask: "If it was already done, what would I do next?" Notice how the answer shifts to something quieter and more graceful. That feeling is aligned action.

3. Lofty Questions

Open-ended questions presupposing the desired future is already here. Said or written daily during meditation. Examples: "Why does money come to me so easily?" / "Why do I have the fit muscular body of an athlete?" These bypass the conscious mind's resistance and reprogram at the subconscious level.

Block 5
The Fuel · Energetic Alignment

Wrong Environment

The physical environment — spaces, people, media — carries a frequency that either amplifies or suppresses the client's ability to manifest.
Core Belief "I'm stuck in circumstances I can't change."

How to Recognize It

  • Low energy in certain spaces; feeling drained at home or work.
  • Spending time with people who diminish dreams or spread negativity.
  • "I feel stuck but I don't know why."
  • "I feel different when I'm in a different space."
  • Consuming fear-based media; tolerating environments that don't inspire.

Common Origins

  • Staying in familiar but draining environments out of comfort, habit, or obligation
  • Not knowing that physical environment affects manifesting capacity
  • "Too good to leave, too bad to stay" relationships

Suggested Exercises

1. The Energy Audit

Have the client rate five categories as "Charges me" or "Drains me":
People (friends, colleagues, partners) • Spaces (go room by room) • Media (news, shows, books) • Digital habits (apps, social media) • Places they frequent
Anything that drains them is an energy leak that suppresses manifesting.

2. Feng Shui Alignment

Recommend the Marie Diamond "Your Diamond Compass" app. Input birthdate, get personal success/love/health directions. Redesign desk and living space to face optimal directions. Reference: Marie Diamond's Mindvalley lesson "Turn Your Home Into A 3D Vision Board."

3. Environment Upgrade Action

Identify one concrete environmental change this week: remove a draining person/media source, rearrange a workspace, or upgrade a room. Start with the highest-drain item from the Energy Audit. Frame investment in environment as investment in manifesting capacity.

Block 6
The GPS · Subconscious Programming

Bullshit Rules (Brules)

Hidden automatic programs from childhood, culture, religion, or trauma that silently govern what the client allows themselves to have. Three subtypes: safety settings, worthiness settings, and association settings.
Core Belief "It's not safe to be wealthy / loved / visible." — "Money is the root of all evil." — "I don't deserve that destination."

How to Recognize It

  • "Money is dangerous / dirty / evil" or "Rich people are bad / greedy."
  • "People like me don't get to have that."
  • "My parents always said…" followed by a limiting belief.
  • Repeating patterns of self-sabotage aligned with cultural or family rules.
  • Automatic negative reactions when money, success, or love topics arise.

Common Origins

  • Childhood programming from parents, teachers, religious institutions
  • Cultural rules absorbed unconsciously
  • A single defining moment that installed the rule (often before age 9)
  • Trauma-based associations (e.g., "success = betrayal" from a specific event)

Suggested Exercises

1. Sentence Completion Exercise

Have the client write quickly without thinking:
"Money is ___" • "Love is ___" • "Success is ___"
Spot negative associations. These are the Brules running the GPS. Discuss where each belief came from.

2. Lofty Questions to Reprogram

Craft a question that rewrites the block. Repeat daily during morning practice for 30 days:
Block "money is evil" → "Why is money always flowing to me in ethical and joyful ways?"
Block "love leads to heartbreak" → "Why do I receive love from all sources in such powerful and enriched ways?"

3. The Clearing Statement

From Christie Marie Sheldon's Unlimited Abundance: Have the client say seven times while visualizing the block shrinking:
"I uncreate, delete the story, this block, across all time, dimension, space, and realities."

Block 7
The GPS · Subconscious Programming

Visualization from Lack

The client can see the dream on their vision board, but emotionally they're broadcasting the gap — not the arrival. The feeling underneath the visualization is absence, not trust.
Core Belief "I want it so badly because I don't have it." (broadcasting absence)

How to Recognize It

  • Doing all the "right" visualization practices but feeling worse after them.
  • Vision boards that trigger frustration rather than inspiration.
  • "I can see it but it feels so far away."
  • Constant awareness of the gap between current reality and desired reality.
  • Longing, yearning, or sadness during visualization exercises.

Common Origins

  • Misunderstanding of how visualization works — taught to visualize the goal without being taught the required emotional state
  • Desperate desire creating energetic resistance

Suggested Exercises

1. Shift the Feeling State

Teach the client to visualize from trust and calm confidence, not longing. The feeling should be "it's coming" not "I need it." Have them practice feeling as though they are remembering a future that has already happened.

2. The Trust Mantra

Adopt and repeat daily: "If it's meant for me, it's not gonna miss me. And if it misses me, it was never meant to be mine." Hold this attitude toward everything on the vision board.

3. Arrival Visualization

Guide the client to visualize as if the goal is already arranged. Instead of reaching forward toward the goal, have them stand in the accomplished version of reality and look around. What do they see? How does it feel? This switches the signal from lack to arrival.

Block 8
The GPS · Subconscious Programming

Time Lag Frustration

Impatience and doubt when progress isn't immediate, creating desperation that actively repels the goal. Per Vadim Zeland: desperation creates "excess potential" that pushes outcomes away.
Core Belief "It's not happening fast enough." / "It should have happened by now."

How to Recognize It

  • Constantly checking for results.
  • Giving up on practices prematurely.
  • Switching methods or programs frequently.
  • "Maybe this manifesting stuff doesn't work."
  • Expressing frustration, desperation, or doubt about timeline.

Common Origins

  • Culture of instant gratification
  • Comparison to others' timelines
  • Misunderstanding manifesting as instant rather than unfolding

Suggested Exercises

1. The "No Big Deal" State

Cultivate calm confidence. The goal becomes a "nice to have" rather than a "must have." High importance creates energetic turbulence. Coach the client to know it is coming without attaching to the exact timeline.

2. Trust Journaling

Each morning, write three proofs that life is unfolding perfectly. Not general gratitude — specific evidence that the universe is orchestrating events in their favor.

3. Lower Importance

Explain Vadim Zeland's concept: assigning high importance to a goal creates excess potential that repels it. Have the client practice holding the goal lightly — wanting it without needing it. The paradox: the less desperately you need it, the faster it arrives.

Block 9
The Driver · Manifesting Integration

Surrender Block

The inability to trust the universe's timing — clinging to how and when a goal must arrive creates resistance and pushes the desired outcome away.
Core Belief "I need to control how and when this happens."

How to Recognize It

  • Over-planning and rigidity about how goals must manifest.
  • Anxiety when things don't go according to plan.
  • Inability to see value in unexpected developments or "detours."
  • "I've done everything right, where are my results?"
  • Fake non-attachment: saying "I don't care" while secretly caring deeply.

Common Origins

  • Need for control; past experiences where trusting led to disappointment
  • Type-A personality conditioning
  • Lack of evidence (yet) that the universe is working on their behalf

Suggested Exercises

1. Mirror Statement (Marisa Peer technique)

Write on the bathroom mirror or a card they carry: "It's on its way in perfect timing." Read it every morning and night. The repetition rewires the nervous system's need for control.

2. Trust Journaling

Each morning, write three specific pieces of evidence that the universe is orchestrating events in their favor. Not general gratitude — specific "breadcrumbs" of synchronicity. This builds a trust archive.

3. The Karate Kid Reframe

Share the Mr. Miyagi analogy: "The universe is just making you wax on and wax off." What seems like frustrating detours are actually precise preparation. Ask: "What if this detour is teaching you exactly what you'll need when the goal arrives?" Also recommend reading The Surrender Experiment by Michael Singer.

Block 10
The Driver · Manifesting Integration

Quantum Leap Syndrome

Overwhelm, panic, or self-sabotage when the manifestation arrives faster and bigger than expected. The nervous system isn't calibrated to receive that level of success, love, or abundance.
Core Belief "This is too much too fast — I can't handle this level of success."

How to Recognize It

  • Panic, overwhelm, or freezing when success arrives.
  • Self-sabotage after receiving what was desired.
  • "This feels too good to be true" / "I feel like a fraud."
  • Turning down opportunities or destroying deals at the moment of success.
  • Physical symptoms (insomnia, illness) when a quantum leap occurs.

Common Origins

  • Nervous system not gradually acclimated to higher levels of success
  • Past experiences of loss after gaining
  • Imposter syndrome; going from scarcity to abundance too quickly

Suggested Exercises

1. Celebrate Small Wins

Not just big milestones. Every small win tells the nervous system "this level is safe." Have the client create a daily practice of acknowledging progress — no matter how small — to gradually expand their capacity for receiving.

2. Progressive Overload (The Puppy Metaphor)

Scale dreams like building muscle. Don't leap from $500K to $10M. Aim for $1M first, understand what life looks like there, then $2M, then $5M. "You don't throw the puppy in the bath. You let it lick water from your hand first." Progressive overload, not shock training.

3. Daily Embodiment Visualization

Guide the client to visualize already inhabiting their goal so identity catches up with reality before reality arrives. Have them step into the body of their future self, notice body tension, soothe it, and receive the message: "I'll see you here soon. Trust the process."
